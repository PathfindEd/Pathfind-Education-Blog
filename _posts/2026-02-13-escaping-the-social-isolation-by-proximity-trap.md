## The Comfort Trap

It is human nature to seek comfort. For international students, this often manifests as quickly forming a tight social circle comprised exclusively of students from their home country or region.

This creates the **Social Isolation by Proximity Trap**. You feel safe and supported, but you have effectively capped your growth.

If your primary social life only exists in your native language, your cultural understanding, your English language skills, and your local professional networking will not advance beyond their starting point.

---

## Build Local Bridges

To truly succeed abroad, you must push yourself to join the obscure, hyper-local clubs.

Do not just join the "International Student Society." Join the Uni History Society, the local cycling group, or the amateur theatre society.

These friendships are often harder to form initially because you do not have the immediate shared background. However, these groups provide essential local bridges. They connect you with domestic students and locals who can offer unique professional opportunities and cultural insights that you will never find inside an expatriate bubble.

Growth happens outside the comfort zone.
